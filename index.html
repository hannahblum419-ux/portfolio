About

Arts & Culture · Strategic Research

Nonprofit Arts Organization

Community visioning and strategic research for a contemporary arts organization planning its next chapter

View project

As the organization ended a three-year strategic plan and gained approval to develop a new facility, the central question was: what do the people who make up this community actually believe this organization is — and what do they want it to become? Research spanned 412 survey responses across five stakeholder groups, artist workshops, and 8 community partner interviews. Findings were synthesized into affinity maps and a community sentiment evaluation, then brought into a one-day visioning workshop with staff and the steering committee. The work directly shaped a board-approved three-year strategic plan.

Each method surfaced something different: surveys captured scale and sentiment, interviews surfaced nuance, and workshops generated ideas that neither could on their own. What I'd do differently: build in a more structured way to triangulate across methods earlier in synthesis, rather than treating each source separately before bringing them together.

Methods Surveys (n=412), stakeholder interviews, artist workshops, affinity mapping, facilitated visioning
Deliverable Research outcomes summary, community sentiment evaluation, three-year strategic plan
My role Researcher, synthesist, workshop co-facilitator
Operations · Systems Design

Commercial Client

Enterprise operations design for a rapidly scaling firm with manual, unsustainable workflows

View project

Document approvals required 3–7 email exchanges. Processes built for a two-person team were breaking under organizational growth. Discovery interviews and process walkthroughs across three departments surfaced the friction points — and informed a system architecture built to scale. For each department, I produced a system architecture recommendation, user permissions matrix, and workflow guide. After building out the platform, I returned for structured review sessions and iterated before finalizing. The result: from 2 users to 30+ daily active users across three departments, a client-funded platform upgrade, and an engagement expanded to two additional departments now heading toward enterprise-wide rollout.

Working across three departments with very different functions made it clear how much context shapes process needs. What I'd do differently: conduct a cross-functional alignment session earlier to surface shared pain points and vocabulary before designing department-specific solutions — it would have reduced iteration time and strengthened adoption.

Methods Stakeholder interviews, process walkthroughs, landscape research, iterative design
Deliverable System architecture docs, permissions matrix, workflow guides, built platform
My role Researcher, systems designer, platform builder
Construction · HR Research

Construction Industry Client

HR process research and redesign across case management, recruiting, and onboarding

View project

An industry-leading contractor needed to document and improve three core HR processes — each branching into sub-processes across professional staff, field staff, and craft employees. I designed interview guides and independently conducted 25+ semi-structured interviews across roles and levels, then translated findings into swimlane process maps for all three processes. Industry benchmarking provided external context. Research surfaced high-impact structural and systemic opportunities across all three process maps, with direct implications for operational efficiency, consistency, and employee experience.

Conducting 25+ interviews across a highly differentiated workforce sharpened my ability to adapt interview style without compromising consistency. The most valuable skill I developed was learning to hold complexity: mapping processes that branch and sub-branch without losing sight of the human experience at each node. What I'd do differently: involve frontline employees earlier in validating process maps before finalizing, rather than relying solely on leadership review.

Methods Semi-structured interviews (25+), process mapping, industry benchmarking
Deliverable Swimlane process maps, recommendations report, leadership presentation
My role Lead researcher, process mapper, presenter
